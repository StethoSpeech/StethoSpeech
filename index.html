StethoSpeech: Speech Generation Through Stethoscopic Microphone Attached To The Skin

Abstract:

StethoSpeech is a Silent Speech Interface (SSI) that captures flesh-conducted vibrations behind the ear and transforms them into speech. This innovation can greatly improve social interactions for those with voice disorders and also enable discreet communication in public. Unlike traditional SSIs, StethoSpeech doesn't require specific speaker-paired data, making it practical for severe medical conditions. The system includes data preparation, a self-supervised speech encoder, a sequence-to-sequence network for modality mapping, and a speech decoder. We introduce a groundbreaking large-scale StethoText dataset, the first of its kind, for transforming speaker-specific vibrations into audible and understandable speech in a natural setting, and advancing voice disorder treatment through signal processing. Furthermore, StethoSpeech excels in experiments, enhancing speech quality, intelligibility, and prosody on the CSTR NAM TIMIT Plus corpus. Speech samples can be found at https://stethospeech.github.io/StethoSpeech/
